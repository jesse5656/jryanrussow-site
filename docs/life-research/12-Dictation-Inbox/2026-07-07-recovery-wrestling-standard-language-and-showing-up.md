Version: 0.1.0

## Purpose

Preserve a dictated life research narrative related to recovery, wrestling, coaching systems, standard terminology, persistence, health, and family stewardship.

## Raw Narrative Summary

This narrative captures the period when Jesse Russow returned to wrestling after approximately twenty years away, recognized deficiencies in local youth coaching systems, and concluded that he would need to teach his children himself.

The narrative connects recovery, sobriety, physical health, sleep apnea, strength training, youth wrestling, coaching systems, Pearler Wrestling, resistance to improved methods, and the repeated importance of showing up.

## Core Observations

- Standard terminology reduces confusion, misunderstanding, safety issues, and wasted time.
- Industry-standard language functions as operational infrastructure.
- Showing up consistently preserves the opportunity for discovery.
- Recovery, health, coaching, and systems improvement were interconnected.
- Structured curricula outperform ad hoc coaching when teaching beginners.
- Good systems are often resisted when trust, culture, or leadership alignment are weak.
- Personal experience should be treated as field data, not proof.

## Purpose

This framework proposes a lifecycle describing how organizational knowledge is created, preserved, transferred, applied, forgotten, and rediscovered.

It represents a working hypothesis that will evolve through research and field validation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Lifecycle

Observation

↓

Capture

↓

Organization

↓

Validation

↓

Application

↓

Transfer

↓

Preservation

↓

Institutional Memory

↓

Continuous Improvement

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Failure Modes

Knowledge may be lost through:

- undocumented work
- employee turnover
- poor communication
- inaccessible systems
- lack of stewardship
- organizational restructuring
- obsolete technology
- cultural resistance

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Working Hypothesis

Organizations do not primarily lose knowledge because people forget.

They lose knowledge because systems fail to preserve and transfer it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Research Questions

- Which stages fail most frequently?
- Which interventions improve preservation?
- How should technology support each stage?
- Which stages require leadership rather than software?

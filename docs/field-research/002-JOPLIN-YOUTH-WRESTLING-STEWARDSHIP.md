# Field Research 002 — Joplin Youth Wrestling Club and Stewardship Transfer

Version: 0.1.0

Status:
Draft Field Entry

## Context

J. Ryan Russow served as Board Vice President for the Joplin Youth Wrestling Club.

During his leadership, standards, systems, and fundraising discipline were implemented.

Approximately $50,000 was raised for the club.

After leadership changed, the systems did not hold and the funds were depleted within several years.

## What Happened

The system worked under effective stewardship.

It failed after stewardship changed.

## What I Believed Before

If the system was right, people should follow it.

A good system should be obvious when the facts support it.

## What I Believed After

Correct systems do not automatically survive.

Systems require stewards.

Culture, incentives, governance, character, trust, and succession determine whether a system survives beyond its original architect.

## Emerging Principle

No system can permanently outperform the character, competence, and commitment of the people entrusted to steward it.

## Possible Theorem

A system that performs under its founder has not necessarily achieved institutional durability.

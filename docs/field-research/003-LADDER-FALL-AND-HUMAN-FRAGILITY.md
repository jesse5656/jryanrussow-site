# Field Research 003 — The Ladder Fall and Human Fragility

Version: 0.1.0

Status:
Draft Field Entry

## Context

After years of building businesses, leading teams, and relying heavily on
personal capability, a ladder fall fundamentally changed the trajectory of
my life.

The physical injury was followed by medical decisions that produced lasting
consequences.

What appeared to be a single accident ultimately revealed weaknesses in
multiple interconnected systems.

## What Happened

The injury affected far more than physical health.

It affected identity.

Decision making.

Business operations.

Leadership.

Relationships.

Financial stability.

Time.

Everything connected.

## What I Believed Before

Hard work could overcome nearly every obstacle.

Capability primarily existed within the individual.

## What I Believed After

Capability is systemic.

When one critical system fails,
many other systems become unstable.

Resilient lives require resilient systems.

## Emerging Principle

Human capability should never depend upon a single point of failure.

## Possible Theorem

The more critical a capability,
the more intentionally it should be protected by redundant systems.

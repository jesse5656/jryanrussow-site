# Case Study 001 — Joplin Youth Wrestling Club

Version: 0.1.0
Status: Draft Case Study

## Context

J. Ryan Russow served as Board Vice President for the Joplin Youth Wrestling Club.

During that period, systems, standards, fundraising, and organizational discipline were implemented.

Approximately $50,000 was raised for the club.

## Observation

The system functioned while effective stewardship was present.

After leadership changed, the system deteriorated and the resources were depleted within several years.

## Initial Lesson

A system may function under its original steward but fail when stewardship changes.

This does not necessarily mean the system itself was defective.

It may mean the system required:

- stronger succession planning
- clearer governance
- deeper cultural adoption
- better steward development
- better alignment among stakeholders

## Key Distinction

System performance under the founder or original steward is not the same as system durability after transition.

## Draft Principle

No system can permanently outperform the character, competence, and commitment of the people entrusted to steward it.

## Draft Theorem

A system that depends entirely on one person may perform well, but it has not yet become institutionally durable.

## Open Questions

- When is it worth continuing to invest in a resistant culture?
- When should a Systems Architect leave and build elsewhere?
- How much political coalition-building is necessary for stewardship?
- Can a volunteer organization maintain high standards without a strong central steward?
- How should systems be designed for leadership turnover?
- What are the minimum cultural conditions required for a system to survive?

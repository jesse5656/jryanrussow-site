# Research Program Execution

Version: 1.0.0

Status:
Active

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Purpose

This document defines the standard operational procedure for executing a research program.

It answers one question:

How does a Russow Institute researcher convert raw information into durable institutional knowledge?

This procedure is independent of subject matter and applies to every research program.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Guiding Principles

Research before architecture.

Evidence before synthesis.

Primary sources before commentary.

Traceability before conclusions.

Frameworks before canon.

Reality should justify theory.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Research Execution Workflow

### Step 1 — Understand the Research Program

Read:

- Research Program README
- Open Questions
- Bibliography
- Existing Frameworks
- Existing Case Studies

Understand the objective before collecting evidence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Step 2 — Select One Primary Source

Choose one primary source.

Ignore secondary commentary until primary evidence has been reviewed.

Examples:

- Government reports
- Court records
- Original research papers
- Interviews
- Internal documentation

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Step 3 — Read for Evidence

Do not read for entertainment.

Identify:

- observations
- failures
- successes
- decisions
- assumptions
- communication breakdowns
- organizational behavior
- recurring patterns

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Step 4 — Capture Evidence

For every significant finding record:

- Finding
- Direct quotation
- Page or location
- Context
- Interpretation
- Questions raised
- Related research questions
- Confidence

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Step 5 — Build Relationships

Connect findings to:

- existing evidence
- frameworks
- case studies
- hypotheses
- papers

Nothing should exist in isolation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Step 6 — Identify Patterns

Look for repeated structures.

Do not force conclusions.

Allow patterns to emerge from evidence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Step 7 — Draft Frameworks

Only after sufficient evidence exists.

Frameworks should explain observed reality.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Step 8 — Draft Papers

Papers synthesize evidence.

They do not create evidence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Step 9 — Evaluate Canon Candidates

Only mature, repeatedly supported principles become canon candidates.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Completion Criteria

A research program iteration is complete when:

- evidence has been captured
- traceability has been established
- bibliography updated
- frameworks revised if necessary
- papers updated if necessary
- operating plan updated
- changes committed

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Philosophy

The repository is the institutional memory.

The researcher is a steward of evidence.

Evidence produces frameworks.

Frameworks produce knowledge.

Knowledge may eventually become canon.

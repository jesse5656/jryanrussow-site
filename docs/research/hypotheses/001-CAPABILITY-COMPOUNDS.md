## Hypothesis

Capability compounds only when knowledge survives the people who created it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Why This Matters

Organizations invest enormous resources acquiring capability.

When knowledge is lost through turnover, poor documentation, weak stewardship, or organizational failure, much of that investment disappears.

If the hypothesis is correct, preserving knowledge should allow capability to compound across people, teams, organizations, and generations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Predictions

If this hypothesis is true, then organizations with stronger knowledge preservation systems should:

- recover from turnover more quickly
- improve more consistently over time
- make fewer repeated mistakes
- require less rediscovery of existing knowledge
- adapt more effectively to change

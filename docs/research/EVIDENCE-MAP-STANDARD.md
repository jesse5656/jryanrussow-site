# Evidence Map Standard

Version: 0.1.0
Status: Draft Standard

## Purpose

Every major claim, framework, and chapter should eventually include an evidence map.

The purpose is to distinguish between:

- lived experience
- repeated observation
- external research
- case studies
- hypotheses
- open questions

## Evidence Categories

### Personal Experience

Evidence drawn from direct lived experience.

### Repeated Observation

Patterns observed across multiple situations or years.

### Case Study

A documented real-world example.

### External Research

Books, studies, academic papers, industry reports, or established literature.

### Historical Example

Evidence drawn from history, institutions, leaders, civilizations, or long-term patterns.

### Competing Theory

A serious alternative explanation.

### Open Question

Something not yet proven or fully understood.

## Rating Scale

★★★★★ Strong evidence  
★★★★ Good evidence  
★★★ Moderate evidence  
★★ Limited evidence  
★ Weak evidence / hypothesis only

## Required Format

Each major chapter should end with:

- Evidence Summary
- What We Know
- What We Believe
- What We Are Testing
- What Could Prove This Wrong

# Foundational Questions

Version: 0.1.0
Status: Living Research Agenda

## Core Question

Is stewardship the central organizing principle of the Systems Architect Philosophy,
or is there a more fundamental concept that better explains the entire body of work?

## Current Hypothesis

Time is finite.

Because time is finite, stewardship matters.

Because stewardship matters, systems are required.

Because systems are required, architecture becomes necessary.

## Working Chain

Time

↓

Stewardship

↓

Systems

↓

Freedom

↓

Legacy

## Questions to Test

- Is time the highest-order constraint?
- Is stewardship the primary moral responsibility created by finite time?
- Are systems the mechanism through which stewardship becomes repeatable?
- Does freedom result from well-designed systems?
- Does legacy emerge from stewardship compounded over time?
- What evidence would prove this model wrong?

# Systems Architect Glossary

Version: 0.1.0
Status: Living Glossary

## System

An intentionally organized set of interdependent elements that transforms inputs into outcomes while pursuing a defined purpose.

## Architecture

The disciplined design of structure, relationships, constraints, and flows so a system can function, adapt, and endure.

## Systems Architecture

The disciplined practice of transforming experience into enduring systems that preserve what matters, reduce unnecessary friction, and create compounding freedom across generations.

## Stewardship

The intentional responsibility to preserve, improve, and faithfully transfer people, knowledge, resources, relationships, and systems so that those who follow inherit greater opportunity than we received.

## Time

The finite, non-renewable resource that gives stewardship urgency.

## Knowledge

Information refined by experience, context, judgment, and transferability.

## Tacit Knowledge

Knowledge that lives in people through experience, skill, judgment, and pattern recognition but has not yet been made transferable.

## Transferability

The degree to which knowledge, skill, judgment, or process can be taught, documented, repeated, improved, and passed forward.

## Freedom

The capacity to consistently choose what matters because essential systems reliably protect time, energy, knowledge, and judgment.

## Legacy

The compounded effect of stewardship over time.

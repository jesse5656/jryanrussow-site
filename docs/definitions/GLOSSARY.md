# Systems Architect Glossary

Version: 0.2.0

Status:
Living Glossary

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## System

An intentionally organized set of interdependent elements that transforms inputs into outcomes while pursuing a defined purpose.

## Architecture

The disciplined design of structure, relationships, constraints, and flows so a system can function, adapt, and endure.

## Systems Architecture

The disciplined practice of designing, documenting, implementing, improving, and transferring systems that preserve what matters and create compounding freedom over time.

## Systems Architect

A steward who intentionally designs, documents, integrates, and continually improves systems that transform knowledge into repeatable outcomes while preserving what matters across generations.

## Stewardship

The intentional responsibility to preserve, improve, and faithfully transfer people, knowledge, resources, relationships, and systems so those who follow inherit greater opportunity than we received.

## Time

The finite, non-renewable resource that gives stewardship urgency.

## Freedom

The capacity to consistently choose what matters because essential systems reliably protect time, energy, knowledge, and judgment.

## Knowledge

Information refined by experience, context, judgment, and transferability.

## Tacit Knowledge

Knowledge that lives in people through experience, skill, judgment, and pattern recognition but has not yet been made transferable.

## Transferability

The degree to which knowledge, skill, judgment, or process can be taught, documented, repeated, improved, and passed forward.

## Capability

The practical capacity to act effectively.

## Human Capability

The combined practical capacity of a person to think, decide, lead, work, adapt, learn, build, and steward what matters.

## Compounding

The process by which repeated small actions, decisions, systems, or improvements accumulate into disproportionate long-term results.

## Entropy

The natural tendency of systems to drift toward disorder, decay, inefficiency, or loss unless intentionally maintained.

## Institutional Durability

The ability of a system, organization, or body of knowledge to continue functioning, improving, and preserving its purpose after its original founder, architect, or steward is no longer directly leading it.

## Human Operating System

The intentional collection of principles, habits, frameworks, feedback loops, and stewardship practices that governs how a person lives, learns, leads, and leaves a legacy.

## Business Operating System

The intentional structure of workflows, roles, knowledge, technology, governance, and feedback loops that allows an organization to create value without depending entirely on heroic individuals.

## Knowledge Operating System

The intentional structure for capturing, refining, organizing, transferring, and preserving knowledge so it can compound beyond the individual.

## Social Operating System

The informal and formal structures of trust, influence, incentives, relationships, governance, and culture that determine how groups adopt or reject systems.

## Framework

A structured model that helps people understand, apply, test, and improve a principle.

## Doctrine

A set of guiding beliefs and operating standards that shape how practitioners think and act.

## Axiom

A foundational assumption treated as true within the discipline unless evidence requires revision.

## Principle

A broadly applicable truth supported by repeated observation, evidence, and logical consistency.

## Hypothesis

A proposed explanation that requires further testing.

## Case Study

A documented real-world example used to examine, test, or illustrate a principle, framework, or hypothesis.

## Evidence Map

A structured summary of what supports a claim, what remains uncertain, and what could challenge or refine the idea.

## Canon

The authoritative body of work containing the current best understanding of the discipline.

## Institute

The stewarding body responsible for preserving, refining, teaching, and improving the discipline.

## Legacy

The compounded effect of stewardship over time.

# Stewardship and Capability

Version: 0.1.0

Status:
Canon Candidate

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Core Principle

Capability compounds only when knowledge survives the people who created it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Supporting Principle

Stewardship is the disciplined preservation and improvement of capability for those who come after us.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Purpose

The Systems Architect Discipline exists to discover, preserve, apply, and transfer capability in ways that outlast individual people, technologies, and organizations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Human Constraint

A system is not successful merely because it creates output, profit, growth, or efficiency.

A system should also preserve what matters most in human life.

The purpose of better systems is not to remove people from life.

The purpose is to reduce unnecessary disorder so people can spend more time on what matters.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Implication

Knowledge should not die with the person who learned it.

Processes should not disappear when the employee leaves.

Organizations should not lose capability every time leadership changes.

Families, businesses, and institutions should be able to inherit more capability than the prior generation received.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Guiding Question

Does this system leave behind more capability than it inherited?

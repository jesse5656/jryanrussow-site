# Research Workflow

Version: 0.1.0

Status:
Field Trial

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Purpose

This workflow describes how ideas mature into enduring knowledge within the Systems Architect Discipline.

It defines the lifecycle of knowledge.

It does not manage daily work.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Workflow

Reality

↓

Observation

↓

Capture

↓

Research

↓

Evidence

↓

Paper

↓

Framework

↓

Field Validation

↓

Canon

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Layer 1 — Reality

Examples include:

- conversations
- meetings
- books
- failures
- successes
- operational experience
- historical research

Reality is the source of all research.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Layer 2 — Capture

Capture takes place in a temporary working environment.

Current implementation:

Obsidian

Purpose:

- Daily Notes
- Research Queue
- Engineering Queue
- Operations Queue
- Brainstorming
- Personal reflection

Information in this layer is expected to change frequently.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Layer 3 — Research

Ideas mature through:

- investigation
- questioning
- evidence gathering
- experimentation
- discussion
- implementation

Research should challenge assumptions rather than confirm them.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Layer 4 — Papers

Research that demonstrates sufficient maturity should become a paper.

Papers remain subject to revision.

They are not considered Canon.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Layer 5 — Canon

Only knowledge that has survived repeated testing, implementation, and revision should enter the Canon.

The Canon represents the current best understanding.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Guiding Principle

Capture quickly.

Research deliberately.

Publish carefully.

Revise continuously.

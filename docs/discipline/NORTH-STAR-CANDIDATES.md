# North Star Candidates

Version: 0.1.0

Status:
Living Strategic Discussion

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Purpose

This document preserves candidate North Star statements for the Systems Architect Discipline.

The objective is not to select one prematurely.

The objective is to allow research, implementation, and experience to determine which statement most accurately describes the Discipline over time.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Evaluation Criteria

A North Star should:

- remain true for decades
- apply across all repositories
- guide difficult decisions
- survive technological change
- communicate the purpose of the Discipline clearly
- improve human and organizational capability

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Candidate 1

Leave behind more capability than you inherited.

Notes:

Simple.

Memorable.

Applies equally to parents, teachers, entrepreneurs, coaches, researchers, organizations, and institutions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Candidate 2

To discover, preserve, apply, and transfer capability.

Notes:

Operational.

Clearly describes the work of the Discipline.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Candidate 3

To discover, preserve, apply, and transfer capability in ways that outlast individual people, technologies, and organizations.

Notes:

Current leading mission statement.

Emphasizes stewardship across generations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Candidate 4

Capability compounds only when knowledge survives the people who created it.

Notes:

Potential Canon Principle.

May ultimately belong in the Canon rather than serve as the North Star.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Candidate 5

Prevent unnecessary rediscovery.

Notes:

Explains the motivation behind Institutional Memory, Midwest24 Archive, documentation, and stewardship.

Potentially powerful because of its simplicity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Candidate 6

Stewardship is the disciplined preservation and improvement of capability for those who come after us.

Notes:

Potential constitutional principle.

Defines stewardship rather than mission.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Candidate 7

Build systems that permanently improve human and organizational capability.

Notes:

Strong engineering emphasis.

Suitable for Midwest24 and operational implementation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Candidate 8

How do people and organizations build capability that survives time, turnover, adversity, and technological change?

Notes:

This may be the central research question rather than the North Star.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Decision Rule

Do not choose a permanent North Star until sufficient research, implementation, and operational validation have accumulated.

The Discipline should discover its North Star rather than invent it.

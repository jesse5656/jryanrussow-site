The same principle applies: obsess over quarterly profits, and you'll make short-term decisions that kill long-term value. Obsess over customer outcomes, team development, and systems, and profitability becomes the natural byproduct. The best businesses compound; they don't just win quarters.

The foundational text was "The Paradox of Success" by John R. O'Neil. Additional influences include "The Power of Habit" by Charles Duhigg, "Extreme Ownership" by Jocko Willink and Leif Babin, "Principles" by Ray Dalio, and materials from the Positive Coaching Alliance.

Winning streaks create fragility. Players become outcome-dependent, afraid of mistakes, and unwilling to take calculated risks. By strategically allowing failure—letting kids struggle in low-stakes games—you condition resilience. The goal isn't to win every game; it's to win when it matters most.
